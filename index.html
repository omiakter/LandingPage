Pedal Power Bike — System Overview
What We're Building

A bike that blends smoothies
and charges your phone

Upgrading an existing pedal-powered prototype with electricity generation, energy storage, and USB charging — all from human power.

⚡

Energy Generation

A DC generator attached to the front wheel drivetrain converts pedalling motion into usable electrical energy, targeting 50–100W of practical output at typical cruising speed.

🔋

Energy Storage

An off-the-shelf charge controller safely routes generated electricity to a battery for storage while pedalling, with a fuse and manual on/off switch for basic safety.

🔌

USB Charging

A 5V USB port connected to the battery lets users charge phones and small devices from stored energy — making the bike a self-contained portable power station.

Project Scope

What we're delivering

The project follows the ANU Systems Engineering Process, focusing on practical, off-the-shelf integration to produce a reliable demonstration system within budget and timeline.

Out of Scope

Battery-powered blender operation · Custom-designed electrical components · Software or digital interfaces · Permanent installation

01

Electrical Generation System

DC generator on the front wheel, measuring output across the pedalling cadence and torque range.

02

Energy Storage System

Battery with charge controller, fuse, and manual switch for safe energy storage during pedalling.

03

USB Charging Output

5V USB port with labelled output capability and operating limitations for device charging.

04

System Testing

Verification of safe operation, usable generator output, and successful USB device charging.

05

Handover Documentation

Wiring diagrams, bill of materials, risk summary, test results, and operating guidelines.
